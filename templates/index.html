Understanding Liver Cirrhosis

Liver cirrhosis is a late stage of scarring (fibrosis) of the liver caused by many forms of liver diseases and conditions. The liver carries out several necessary functions, including detoxifying harmful substances in your body, cleaning your blood and making vital nutrients.

Early detection and intervention are crucial for managing this condition effectively. Our prediction system uses advanced algorithms to assess risk factors and provide valuable insights for healthcare professionals.

Common Causes

  • Chronic alcohol abuse
  • Hepatitis B and C infections
  • Fatty liver disease
  • Genetic digestive disorders
  • Autoimmune hepatitis
  • Bile duct diseases

Warning Signs

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Easy bleeding or bruising
  • Loss of appetite
  • Nausea and weight loss
  • Swelling in legs and abdomen
  • Jaundice (yellowing of skin)

Treatment Options

  • Lifestyle modifications
  • Medication for complications
  • Treatment of underlying causes
  • Management of portal hypertension
  • Liver transplantation (severe cases)
  • Regular monitoring and follow-up
